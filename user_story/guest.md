# Guest User Journey: Discovering Library Resources

**Scenario: A prospective student exploring the university library before enrollment**

1. **Initial Website Visit**
   - Visitor lands on the library homepage
   - Browses featured books and announcements
   - Notices the clean, responsive design and easy navigation

2. **Exploring the Catalog**
   - Uses the search bar to find books on their subject of interest
   - Filters results by availability, publication year, and format
   - Clicks on book covers to view detailed information
   - Discovers related books through recommendations

3. **Learning About Services**
   - Reads about membership options and borrowing privileges
   - Views information about study spaces and computer access
   - Checks library hours and location details

4. **Accessing Digital Resources**
   - Browses the institutional repository for open-access theses
   - Downloads a sample research paper related to their field
   - Learns about available e-journals and databases

5. **Planning a Visit**
   - Explores the virtual tour to familiarize with library layout
   - Views photos of study areas, reading rooms, and facilities
   - Notes locations of specific collections they're interested in

6. **Engaging with Community**
   - Reads blog posts about library events and resources
   - Views upcoming workshops and seminars
   - Browses the staff directory to identify subject specialists

7. **Getting Help**
   - Finds contact information for research assistance
   - Uses the contact form to ask about graduate program resources
   - Receives an automated response with additional information

8. **Registration Decision**
   - Feels informed about library services and resources
   - Notes the ease of access to information
   - Decides to register as a member upon enrollment
